There is a belief I held about technology strategy for most of the last decade that I have spent the last year revising. It was not a casual position. I have made decisions based on it, argued for it in rooms where it mattered, and used it as a navigating assumption through three different organisations. It was also, I now think, wrong in a specific and consequential way.

I am writing about it because year-end pieces that take stock without actually taking stock — that survey the year in digestible observations, that conclude with something generative about the future — are not useful. The version of this piece that would be easy to write is not the one worth reading.

What follows is an account of what I believed, what happened, and what I think now.
